If I were a real estate agent tracking buyers’ transactions (e.g., in a CRM, pipeline dashboard, or custom tool like a Replit-built app, Trello, or Follow Up Boss), I’d use a streamlined 5-stage Kanban-style board focused on the buyer’s active transaction journey—from initial engagement to post-closing. This keeps things practical, actionable, and focused on what I control as the buyer’s agent: qualifying, searching, contracting, due diligence, and closing.
The stages emphasize progress tracking, task checklists (e.g., documents, deadlines), and bottlenecks (like inspection issues or loan delays). Each “card” represents one buyer/client (or sometimes one specific property if they’re pursuing multiple).
Here are the 5 key stages I’d set up:
1.  Lead / Qualified Buyer This is the entry point for new or warming leads who are serious about buying.
	•  Focus: Onboarding, qualification, and commitment.
	•  Key tasks: Sign buyer representation agreement, gather needs/wants list, get pre-approval letter from lender, set search criteria/budget/timeline, initial consultation/call to build rapport.
	•  Why this stage? Many leads drop off here if not qualified quickly—track metrics like contact attempts and agreement signed.
	•  Kanban indicators: Labels for “Pre-approved” or “Hot lead”; move to next once agreement is signed and they’re actively searching.
2.  Active Search / Showing The house-hunting phase—buyers are viewing properties and narrowing options.
	•  Focus: Property discovery and feedback.
	•  Key tasks: Schedule/conduct showings/open houses, send listing alerts, tour properties, gather feedback after each viewing, refine search (e.g., adjust price range or features), help prioritize shortlist.
	•  This stage can last weeks/months; high activity here.
	•  Kanban tip: Sub-tasks or attachments for showing notes/photos; use WIP limits to avoid overloading with too many active searches.
3.  Offer Submitted / Under Negotiation Transition to commitment—buyer has selected “the one” and we’re in play.
	•  Focus: Crafting and negotiating the deal.
	•  Key tasks: Prepare/submit offer (price, contingencies, earnest money), handle counteroffers/back-and-forth, negotiate repairs/concessions if needed, get offer accepted (ratified contract).
	•  Critical emotional/financial stage; track deadlines for response times.
	•  Kanban indicators: Color-code for “Contingent” or “Multiple offers”; urgent flags for expiring contingencies.
4.  Under Contract / Contingency Period Post-acceptance due diligence—most deals win or lose here.
	•  Focus: Verifying the property and securing financing.
	•  Key tasks: Order/schedule home inspection (and follow-up on issues), appraisal coordination, secure final loan commitment, title search/review, order homeowners insurance, negotiate repairs/credits if inspection reveals problems, satisfy contingencies (financing, inspection, sale of prior home), remove contingencies.
	•  This is where I spend the most coordination time—track vendor deadlines tightly.
	•  Kanban tip: Strict WIP (1–2 per buyer max); checklists for each contingency; alerts for “days on contract.”
5.  Closing / Closed Final push to keys-in-hand and beyond.
	•  Focus: Execution and wrap-up.
	•  Key tasks: Coordinate final walkthrough, review closing disclosure, attend closing/sign docs, fund the purchase, record deed, handle post-closing items (utilities transfer, welcome packet, thank-you/follow-up for referral). Move to “Closed-Won” for success tracking or “Lost” if it falls through.
	•  Post-closing: Nurture for testimonials, reviews, future business (e.g., move-up buyers).
	•  Kanban indicators: “Closed” column with archive option; celebrate wins!
Why exactly 5 stages?
It mirrors common real estate pipelines (pre-approval/search → offer/negotiation → contingencies/due diligence → closing) while keeping the board simple and visual—too many columns lead to confusion, too few hide key risks. This setup aligns with buyer-focused flows from sources like Shaker.io, Paperless Pipeline, and agent CRMs (e.g., separating search from transaction).
Practical enhancements for tracking:
•  Custom fields: Buyer name, target price, pre-approval status, contract date, estimated close date, priority.
•  Automations: Move card on “offer accepted” → trigger inspection reminders; email templates for each stage.
•  Reporting: Track average time per stage, conversion rates (e.g., Qualified → Offer Submitted), drop-off reasons.
